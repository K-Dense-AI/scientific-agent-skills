# Evidence Standards

## Claim Labels

Use these labels in scientific outputs when helpful:

- **Observed**: directly measured in the cited study or local result file.
- **Replicated**: observed across independent datasets, systems, or studies.
- **Consistent with**: compatible with a mechanism but not decisive.
- **Suggests**: moderate inference with limitations.
- **Hypothesis**: plausible but untested or indirect.
- **Speculative**: low-confidence idea requiring validation.
- **Unsupported**: not backed by current evidence.

## Evidence Hierarchy

Match confidence to design:

- randomized controlled trial or well-powered perturbation experiment: stronger causal support when controls are adequate
- prospective cohort: temporal association, vulnerable to confounding
- case-control/cross-sectional: association, limited temporality
- animal/in vitro/organoid: mechanism support, limited human generalizability
- omics screen: discovery/prioritization unless validated
- review/editorial/preprint: context, not primary proof

## Red Flags

Surface these explicitly:

- small sample without effect-size uncertainty
- uncorrected multiple comparisons
- post hoc subgroup claims
- missing randomization, blinding, or allocation concealment
- unclear sample labels or batch structure
- proxy endpoints treated as clinical outcomes
- cross-species or cross-tissue generalization without validation
- correlation framed as causation
- selective citation of supportive papers
- methods unavailable or irreproducible

## Statistical Reporting

Prefer:

- effect size plus confidence interval
- exact test/model and assumptions
- sample size by group
- correction method for multiplicity
- missing data handling
- sensitivity checks

Avoid:

- "significant" without effect size
- "no effect" from an underpowered non-significant result
- averages without variance
- figures whose axis/units do not match the table
